SEO Basics: How to Get Found on Google

Search Engine Optimization (SEO) is the practice of improving your website so that search engines like Google rank it higher in search results.

[image: https://picsum.photos/seed/seocrawler/760/380 | A visual showing how search engine crawlers discover web pages]

## How Search Engines Work

Search engines use automated programs called crawlers to browse the web, discover pages, and add them to an index.

The three pillars of what search engines evaluate are:
- Relevance: Does your content actually answer the searcher's question?
- Authority: Do other reputable websites link to yours?
- Experience: Is your site fast, secure, and easy to use on mobile?

## Keyword Research

Keywords are the words and phrases people type into search engines. Your job is to identify which keywords your audience uses and create content that addresses those queries.

[image: https://picsum.photos/seed/keywords/760/340 | An illustration of keyword research showing search volume and competition]

## On-Page Optimization

Once you have your keywords, use them naturally in these key places:
- The page title
- The main heading (H1)
- The first paragraph
- Image alt text
- The URL slug

## Technical SEO Basics

Your site must be fast. Pages that take more than three seconds to load lose a significant portion of visitors. Your site must also be mobile-friendly and served over HTTPS.

## Building Authority

Authority comes from other websites linking to yours. Earn backlinks by creating content worth linking to: original research, comprehensive guides, useful tools, or unique perspectives.

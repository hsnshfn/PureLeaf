SEO Basics: How to Get Found on Google

Search Engine Optimization (SEO) is the practice of improving your website so that search engines like Google rank it higher in search results. When someone searches for a topic related to your business, you want to appear on the first page — ideally in the top three results.

[image: https://picsum.photos/seed/seocrawler/760/380 | A visual showing how search engine crawlers discover web pages]

## How Search Engines Work

Search engines use automated programs called "crawlers" or "spiders" to browse the web, discover pages, and add them to an index. When someone performs a search, the engine looks through this index and ranks pages based on hundreds of factors.

The three pillars of what search engines evaluate are:
- Relevance: Does your content actually answer the searcher's question?
- Authority: Do other reputable websites link to yours?
- Experience: Is your site fast, secure, and easy to use on mobile?

## Keyword Research

Keywords are the words and phrases people type into search engines. Your job is to identify which keywords your audience uses and create content that addresses those queries.

Start with simple tools like Google Search itself. Type a topic and look at the autocomplete suggestions — those are real searches people make. Also look at the "People Also Ask" boxes and "Related Searches" at the bottom of results pages.

Long-tail keywords — longer, more specific phrases — are often easier to rank for and attract more qualified visitors. "Digital marketing tips for restaurants" is far more specific than "marketing tips," and whoever searches it is much closer to being your ideal customer.

[image: https://picsum.photos/seed/keywords/760/340 | An illustration of keyword research showing search volume and competition]

## On-Page Optimization

Once you have your keywords, use them naturally in these key places:
- The page title (inside the HTML title tag)
- The main heading (H1)
- The first paragraph of your content
- Image file names and alt text
- The URL slug of the page

Never stuff keywords unnaturally. Write for humans first. Google's algorithms are sophisticated enough to understand context, and keyword stuffing will hurt more than help.

## Technical SEO Basics

Your site must be fast. Pages that take more than three seconds to load lose a significant portion of visitors. Compress your images, minimize unnecessary code, and use a reliable hosting provider.

Your site must be mobile-friendly. More than half of all web searches now happen on mobile devices. Google uses mobile-first indexing, meaning it primarily uses the mobile version of your site for ranking.

Install an SSL certificate so your site uses HTTPS. This is a basic trust and security signal that search engines factor into rankings.

## Building Authority

Authority comes from other websites linking to yours. These backlinks act as votes of confidence. One link from a highly respected website in your industry is worth far more than dozens of links from low-quality sources.

Earn backlinks by creating content worth linking to: original research, comprehensive guides, useful tools, or unique perspectives. Reach out to relevant websites and publications to share your content with them.

SEO is a long game. Results typically take three to six months to appear. But the traffic you earn through SEO is free, sustainable, and compounds over time — making it one of the most valuable long-term investments a business can make.
